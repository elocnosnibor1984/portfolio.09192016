Silicon Valley Faces

AboutThis is a social media website for the non-profit Silicon Valley Faces, which teaches leadership skills to High Schoolers, also with awareness of social issues such as bullying, LGBT sensitivity, and emotional abuse. The site was designed to help the organizers keep in touch with graduates/alumni and other stakeholders in the program.
What I LearnedA great deal of what I learned on this project deals with working in a team. There were 8 of us, and learning to deal with merge conflicts, commenting code, writing clearly, using good naming conventions, refactoring code and using includes to make the files more readable were all key. In addition, this project was developed according to the Scrum/Agile methodology, and I found the experience of breaking the project into tasks which tracked with sticky notes on the Sprint Backlog to be valuable experience as well. And let's not forget about the value of testing, deploying increments of the product, and then testing again to make sure it really works once it's out of production.
TechnologiesMongoDB, Express, Angular, Nodejs, Angular-Bootstrap, Regex

Tiger Monkey Paw

AboutA few years ago I was training Brazilian Jiu Jitsu (a kind of submission wrestling), and I was trying to teach myself techniques from youtube, but there are so many youtube videos which teach so many things - even contradictory things, that I was having a hard time keeping track of what was important. I created this website to solve that problem. It allows you to add the video to your account via the url, to give a star rating, to put it on a list of related videos, to give it an order on that list, and to keep notes on the video such as the most important time segments to watch.
What I LearnedThis was by far my most UX centered development experience. I went through several drafts as I condensed the site into a single page applicaion and pushed through the technical challenges that I experienced as a result (it was my first time making a single page application). I was especially happy with the way I manipulated the url so that users could pull directly from the browser window without having to use the "Share" buttons and clicking "embed" to get a usable version of the video. I also liked writing the regex expression used in the search function, and the algorithms that change the position value of every video in the list when a video is deleted.
TechnologiesMongoDB, Express, Angular, Nodejs, Angular-Bootstrap, Regex

Dragonfly Spanish

AboutThere are a lot of people who frequently don't have time to make it to the gym for a workout and don't own any exercise equipment. For this group project we wanted to help these people stay in shape by offering a number of plyometric workouts to choose from, helping keep track of the workouts they've done, and see their friend's workouts and have their workouts be seen by their friends for accountability.
What I LearnedMy big take away from this project was setting up login/registration for the MEAN stack. This was my primary focus. I also implemented the recording workouts and "find a friend" features. This was also an opportunity to begin thinking seriously about questions of layout and user experience - our first few attempts at designing this site resulted in key features being hidden behind menus, not out and available for someone who has just finished a workout.
TechnologiesMongoDB, Express, Angular, Nodejs, Angular-Boostrap
